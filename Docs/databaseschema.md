# Database Schema & Models

The Ecosphere platform relies on a MongoDB database. Below is the documentation of all the Mongoose models used to manage the Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) data, as well as the gamification and user roles[cite: 1].

## 1. Core User & Organization Models
*   **Employee**: Stores employee credentials, personal information, role-based access, and overall ESG/Gamification score[cite: 1].
*   **Department**: Represents organizational departments, tracking department-level ESG scores and carbon limits[cite: 1].

## 2. Environmental Models
*   **CarbonTransaction**: Logs carbon emissions, credits, and offsets generated by departments or specific activities[cite: 1].
*   **EmissionFactor**: Standardized metrics used to calculate the carbon footprint of various activities or assets[cite: 1].
*   **EnvironmentalGoal**: Long-term environmental targets set by the organization or specific departments (e.g., Net Zero by 2030)[cite: 1].
*   **ProductESGProfile**: Tracks the environmental impact and ESG compliance of specific products managed by the company[cite: 1].

## 3. Social & CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) Models
*   **CSRActivity**: Defines corporate social responsibility events (e.g., tree planting, community service) and tracks event details[cite: 1].
*   **DiversityMetric**: Stores data regarding workplace diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) statistics[cite: 1].
*   **EmployeeParticipation**: Records employee attendance and involvement in various CSR activities[cite: 1].

## 4. Governance & Compliance Models
*   **Audit**: Logs system audits, governance reviews, and financial/environmental verifications[cite: 1].
*   **ComplianceIssue**: Tracks flagged governance issues, regulatory breaches, or overdue compliances[cite: 1].
*   **ESGConfig**: Global configuration settings for ESG metrics and organizational standards[cite: 1].
*   **ESGPolicy**: Defines corporate policies related to governance and sustainability[cite: 1].
*   **PolicyAcknowledgement**: Tracks which employees or departments have signed and acknowledged specific corporate ESG policies[cite: 1].

## 5. Gamification & Engagement Models
*   **Challenge**: Sustainability challenges created by admins for employees to participate in (e.g., "Bike to Work Week")[cite: 1].
*   **ChallengeParticipation**: Tracks an employee's progress and completion status for active challenges[cite: 1].
*   **Badge**: Defines the digital badges that can be earned through sustainable actions[cite: 1].
*   **EmployeeBadge**: A mapping model that links earned badges to specific employees[cite: 1].
*   **Reward**: The catalog of redeemable items or perks employees can claim with their earned ESG points[cite: 1].
*   **RewardRedemption**: Logs when an employee trades their points for a specific reward[cite: 1].

## 6. System & Utility Models
*   **Category**: A shared taxonomy model to classify activities, policies, or rewards[cite: 1].
*   **Notification**: Stores system alerts, challenge updates, and compliance warnings for users[cite: 1].
